The best dashboard will help answer questions like:
	•	Which transactions are most likely fraudulent?
	•	Which merchants, locations, or devices show risk?
	•	Do fraud cases happen more often at unusual times or after failed transactions?
	•	How do transaction amount, distance from home, and account balance relate to fraud?
	
	
Calculated Fields and Labels:
	•	 Is Fraud 
	•	 IF fraud_label = 1 THEN "Fraud" ELSE "Not Fraud" END 
	•	 Fraud Count 
	•	 SUM(IF fraud_label = 1 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) 
	•	 Fraud Rate 
	•	 SUM(IF fraud_label = 1 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) / COUNT(fraud_label) 
	•	 Transaction Hour 
	•	If  transaction_time  is a proper time field, use  DATEPART('hour', transaction_time) 
	•	 High Risk Flag 
	•	Example: flag rows where  distance_from_home  is high,  failed_transaction_count  is above 0, or  unusual_time_transaction  is true.
A simple risk score can also help:
	•	 Risk Score 
	•	Give points for  previous_fraud_count ,  is_international_transaction ,  is_new_merchant ,  failed_transaction_count , and high  distance_from_home .
	
	
	
Build the core worksheets:

You should create one worksheet for each major question. This keeps the dashboard clean and easy to manage.
A. KPI summary sheet
Create a top row of key numbers:
	•	Total transactions.
	•	Fraud rate.
	•	Total fraudulent transactions.
	•	Average transaction amount.
	•	Average distance from home.
	•	Number of customers.
Use big, clear number tiles so someone can understand the dataset at a glance.
B. Fraud over time
This chart shows whether fraud increases or decreases over time. Put  transaction_date  on Columns and count of rows on Rows, then color by  fraud_label . A line chart works well here.
C. Fraud by merchant category
Put  merchant_category  on Rows and count of transactions on Columns. Color by fraud rate or fraud count. This helps identify risky business categories.
D. Fraud by transaction amount
Use a box plot or histogram for  transaction_amount_in_million , split by  fraud_label . This helps compare whether fraud transactions tend to be larger or smaller than normal ones.
E. Fraud by distance from home
Use  distance_from_home  on Rows and  fraud_label  on Columns with a box plot. This is useful because fraud often happens at unusual locations compared with the customer’s home location.
F. Time-of-day fraud heatmap
Use  Transaction Hour  and day of week to create a heatmap. Color by fraud count or fraud rate. This is excellent for spotting unusual time patterns.
G. Merchant or device risk table
Create a ranked table showing:
	•	 merchant_id 
	•	 merchant_category 
	•	 device_id 
	•	 ip_address 
	•	fraud count
	•	fraud rate
	•	average transaction amount
